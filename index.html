Vietnamese Pronoun Guide

A quick beginner-safe reference for “I” and “you” in everyday conversations.

Same age / unsuretôi / bạn = safe and neutral. mình / bạn = friendlier and casual.
Younger personIf you are male: anh for yourself. If female: chị. Use em for the younger person.
Older maleanh = slightly older. chú = uncle-age. bác = parent-age/respected older adult. ông = elderly man.
Older femalechị = slightly older. cô = aunt/teacher-age. bác = older respected adult. bà = elderly woman.
Teachersthầy for male teacher, cô for female teacher. Add ạ to sound respectful.
Avoid earlymày / tao can sound rude or very intimate. Avoid with strangers, elders, teachers, and service workers.
